Tim started his career working in Nature Conservation in South Africa, where he developed a deep appreciation for the intersection of technology and environmental stewardship. His journey into open source GIS began with a passion for making geospatial tools accessible to everyone.

In 2008, Tim founded Linfiniti Consulting, which later evolved into Kartoza in 2014 when he partnered with Gavin Fleming. Under his leadership, Kartoza has grown into a respected name in the open source geospatial community.

Tim is recognized globally for his contributions to QGIS, having been awarded the prestigious Perpetual Honorary PSC Member status in 2018. This honor acknowledges his decades of dedication to the project and the wider open source geospatial ecosystem.

His focus on humanitarian, social upliftment, and environmental projects reflects Kartoza's core mission of using technology for positive impact. Tim believes that open source software and open data policies are essential for creating a more equitable and sustainable world.

When not coding or strategizing, Tim can be found exploring the beautiful landscapes of Portugal, where he now resides, always thinking about how maps can tell better stories.
